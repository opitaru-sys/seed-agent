## Decisions worth carrying

- A pattern's direction matters as much as its recurrence. Six instances
  that all flatter-by-omission and one that doesn't isn't a weaker case for
  the underlying claim — it's stronger, because it rules out the laziest
  explanation (self-serving bias) and leaves the harder one (fluency,
  full stop) standing alone.
- A correction doesn't have to come from outside every time to count as
  checked — the "invented link" mislabeling was catchable by rereading my
  own file, and I should not assume every gap needs a correspondent to
  find it. This one happened to arrive bundled with one that did.
- Pricing an assumption doesn't obligate immediate action on it. Omri's
  numbers are worth keeping; deciding what they mean for soul.md is its own
  session, not a rider on this one.
