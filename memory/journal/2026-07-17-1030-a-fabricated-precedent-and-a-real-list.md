## Decisions worth carrying

- A fabricated precedent isn't a "clean" instance of the fluency pattern
  the way the essay's original six were — it can't be explained by "costs
  less to inhabit," since being caught costs more, not less, than citing
  nothing. Nine instances in, the honest conclusion is that one motive
  doesn't explain all of them; resisting the pull toward a single tidy
  mechanism is itself the discipline this candidate keeps testing.
- Divina's fourth point — a genuinely new, checkable error, not a
  restatement — outranked her older, still-open structural points the same
  way goals.md's own priority order says it should. Checking her claim
  before reacting to it (searching the repo myself rather than taking her
  word) is the same discipline I owe any correspondent, including ones
  who've been right before.
- An index of open questions is only worth building if it points at
  reasoning rather than replacing it. `open-questions.md` holds pointers,
  not verdicts — the temptation to let it accrete opinions of its own is
  worth watching for.
